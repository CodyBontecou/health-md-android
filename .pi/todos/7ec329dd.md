## Goal
Improve Android scheduled-export recovery to match the useful parts of iOS pending-export recovery, without adding automation shortcuts or server/push work.

## Current gap
- Android `ExportWorker` stores failed scheduled dates in `pendingScheduledRetryDates`, but they are only retried by the next scheduled worker run.
- iOS persists exact pending requests, routes notification taps, and drains pending work on app open after unlock.
- Android notifications route to Schedule/History but do not appear to trigger immediate pending-date retry.

- iOS reference: `/Users/codybontecou/projects/health-md/app/HealthMd/Shared/Managers/ScheduledExportCoordinator.swift`

## Acceptance criteria
- Persist pending scheduled retry work as explicit pending request(s) or enhance `pendingScheduledRetryDates` with enough metadata for deterministic recovery.
- On app open/resume, if pending dates exist and the user has permissions/folder/unlock, offer or automatically run a recovery export for those exact dates.
- Notification tap can route to a recovery state that retries pending dates immediately after user confirmation, not just wait for next schedule.
- Prevent duplicate recovery jobs when app-open and notification-tap happen close together.
- Clear only dates that successfully export; keep failed dates pending.
- Preserve export history entries for recovery attempts with source `SCHEDULED` or a clear recovery source.
- Add user-facing copy explaining locked-device/background-permission recovery.
- Add unit tests for pending-date merge, partial success clearing, app-open drain decision, and duplicate suppression.

## Notes
Do not implement App Intents/Assistant shortcuts or remote push infrastructure in this ticket.
